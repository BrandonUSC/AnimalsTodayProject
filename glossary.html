Glossary of Terms

Key definitions to understand the wild.

Carnivore

An animal whose diet consists mainly or exclusively of meat (flesh).

Example: Wolf, Tiger.
Herbivore

An animal that feeds primarily on plants.

Example: Deer, Elephant.
Habitat

The natural environment in which an organism lives, or the environment occupied by a species.

Example: Forest, Ocean, Desert.
Omnivore

An animal whose diet consists of both plants and animals.

Example: Bear, Raccoon, Human.
Vertebrate

An animal possessing a backbone or spinal column (e.g., mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish).

Example: Dog, Eagle, Snake.
Invertebrate

An animal lacking a backbone, such as an arthropod, mollusk, or worm.

Example: Octopus, Spider, Beetle.
Endothermic

Referring to a "warm-blooded" animal that is capable of maintaining a constant body temperature largely independent of the external temperature.

Example: Mammals, Birds.
Ectothermic

Referring to a "cold-blooded" animal whose internal body temperature varies considerably, often matching the temperature of the surrounding environment.

Example: Reptiles, Amphibians, Fish.
Camouflage

Structural or behavioral adaptations that allow an animal to blend in with its surroundings, hiding it from predators or prey.

Example: Chameleon, Arctic Fox.
Nocturnal

Active at night and sleeping during the day.

Example: Owl, Bat.
