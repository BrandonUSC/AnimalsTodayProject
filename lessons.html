The 5 Main Vertebrate Classes

Explore the major classifications of animals within Phylum Chordata (vertebrates).

Core Classification Lessons

1. Mammalia ü¶Å

Warm-blooded, hairy, live-bearing animals.

Characterized by **mammary glands** to nurse their young, the presence of **hair or fur**, and being **warm-blooded**. This group is incredibly diverse, including humans, whales, and bats.

2. Aves (Birds) ü¶ú

Feathered, winged, egg-laying vertebrates.

The defining feature is **feathers**. Birds are **warm-blooded**, lay hard-shelled eggs, and have a lightweight skeleton adapted for flight.

3. Reptilia (Reptiles) üêç

Cold-blooded animals with scales or dry skin.

Reptiles are **cold-blooded** (ectothermic) and typically lay eggs on land. They are characterized by **dry, scaly skin** that helps prevent water loss.

4. Amphibia (Amphibians) üê∏

Dual-life animals living in water and on land.

They are **ectothermic** (cold-blooded) and generally undergo **metamorphosis**, starting as water-dwelling larvae (tadpoles) and transitioning to air-breathing adults.

5. Osteichthyes (Bony Fish) üê†

The most diverse group of fish with skeletons made of bone.

The vast majority of fish species belong to this group. They are defined by a skeleton primarily composed of **bone** and possess a **swim bladder** for buoyancy.
